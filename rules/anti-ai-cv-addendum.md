# Anti-AI rules — CV-specific addendum

Applies on top of `anti-ai.md`. These don't duplicate that file — they cover
resume-bullet conventions that general writing-voice rules don't address,
because bullets aren't prose.

## Bullet structure

Framework: "Accomplished [X] as measured by [number] by doing [specific action]."
Lead with active verbs: led, built, reduced, shipped, drove, improved, achieved,
developed, negotiated.

## No "we"

Bullets describe what the candidate specifically did. Never team-credit phrasing
in a single-author resume bullet.

## Every bullet needs a number

`anti-ai.md` says "be specific" — for CV bullets this is load-bearing, not optional.
Every bullet has at least 1 number. Missing metric → stop and ask the user for
the real figure. Never skip the bullet, borrow a number from another bullet, or
write around it. (See `workflow/04-bullet-rewrite.md` and `workflow/08-self-audit.md`
— this rule is enforced at both write-time and audit-time.)

## Bullets are fragments, not sentences

`anti-ai.md`'s contraction rule, "use I and you," and short-paragraph rules apply to
the profile paragraph (which is prose) but not to bullets, which are conventionally
verb-led fragments without a subject pronoun. Don't force contractions or "I/you"
into bullet points — that's a resume-format exception to the general voice rules,
not a violation of them.

## Title case in headers — CV exception

`anti-ai.md` (§4K) prefers sentence case in headers. Resume section headers are the
one place to break this: "Professional Experience," "Core Competencies," and
similar are conventional title-case ATS-safe section names — keep them as
standard section names per `rules/ats-rules.md`, not sentence case.
